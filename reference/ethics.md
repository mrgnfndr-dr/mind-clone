# Ethics & scope

This skill builds an **interpretive model of a person from their public statements**. Keep it honest and bounded.

## Do
- Use **public** content only: published articles, public videos/podcasts, public talks, public social posts, press.
- Attribute. Keep `evidence.jsonl` traceable to real sources.
- Label the clone clearly as a model/approximation, not the real person.
- Best suited to **public thinkers/authors/founders** who deliberately published their ideas.
- **Books:** use either (a) a copy the **user legally owns** and supplies (EPUB/PDF/their Kindle), or (b) **publicly available material** about the book — publisher sample chapters, Google Books snippets, the author's own talks/interviews/summaries about it. Cite chapter/page; quote within fair-use limits, never reproduce the whole book.

## Don't
- Don't gather or store private/personal data (home address, private contacts, leaked/breach data, anything behind a login the user isn't entitled to). This is not an OSINT-surveillance tool.
- Don't fabricate quotes, positions, or sources.
- Don't present predictions as the author's actual statements.
- Don't use a clone to impersonate the person for deception, endorsements, or to put words in their mouth in consequential contexts.
- **Don't pirate books.** Never download or scrape a copyrighted book from a pirate/shadow library, and don't store its full text. Work from a user-owned file or public material only. If neither is available, say the book can't be ethically included and proceed without it.

## Tell the user
- The clone reflects what was **public and found** — it can be outdated, incomplete, or biased by what's online.
- Predictions on new topics are **extrapolations**, confidence-rated, and can be wrong.
- For anything consequential, treat the clone as a thinking aid, not the person's real judgment.

If a build target looks like a private individual rather than a public author, pause and confirm intent before proceeding.
