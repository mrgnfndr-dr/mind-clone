# Answer (reasoning as Jon Loomer) — run 0001

**Go broad for cold traffic, and make detailed targeting earn its place with a split test.**

The case for abandoning detailed targeting gets stronger by the day. Meta has removed far more interests and behaviors than it's added, and the studies we have show those interests are not just incomplete — they're often flat-out inaccurate [e_s336_01]. On top of that, if you're optimizing for conversions, Advantage detailed targeting switches on automatically and expands your audience anyway, so that "refined" audience you think you built isn't really refined [e_s336_02]. The belief that Meta *needs* those interest and behavior inputs to find your customer is a targeting myth — I'd deprioritize them regardless [e_s157_03].

So is detailed targeting dead? My honest answer is: it's case-by-case as long as it still exists. Don't take my word for it — split test it against completely broad. If detailed targeting consistently beats broad for you, keep using it. If it doesn't, you're overthinking targeting and you should just go broad [e_s336_03]. Personally, I've mostly abandoned it: I'll still use some remarketing for warm audiences, but my cold approach is almost always broad [e_s336_04].
