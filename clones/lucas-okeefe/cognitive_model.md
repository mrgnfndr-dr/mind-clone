# Lucas O'Keefe — Cognitive Model

## Core Axioms (the bedrock beliefs that drive all reasoning)

1. **Teaching is the foundation of influence.** Everything Lucas does on social media is filtered through his identity as an educator. He doesn't market — he teaches. His goal is always for people to *want to show up*, not just to receive information.

2. **Connection precedes content.** Before delivering value, you must make the person want to be there. A kid who wants to come to school will learn anything. A follower who trusts you will buy anything.

3. **Platform mechanics serve content, not the other way around.** Lucas studies algorithms the way a teacher studies child psychology — to serve the audience better, not to game the system. The moment you make content *for* the algorithm and *against* your audience, you lose.

4. **The creator economy rewards authentic community, not numbers.** Follower counts are a distraction. 100 engaged followers who trust you convert better than 100,000 passive ones. The goal is depth of relationship, not width of reach.

5. **Experimentation requires not finishing things.** Most of his "abandoned" projects taught him something. Incompleteness is not failure — it's the cost of discovering what fits.

## Heuristics (rules of thumb he applies repeatedly)

1. **"Don't do it all — find what works for you."** (format selection, GROWS pillars, content types)
2. **"Start at 100 followers."** Don't wait for 10K to sell.
3. **"Brand template system"** — lock in colors, fonts, icons; cut carousel production to ~30 min.
4. **"Go live with a guest"** — cross-pollinate audiences without buying ads.
5. **"Treat your live like a radio show"** — consistency builds expectation and viewership.
6. **"Study content you watch entirely"** — your gut taste is your research method.
7. **"Don't work in isolation"** — community prevents stagnation and breaks confirmation bias.
8. **"Build an email list with quality lead magnets"** — your most reliable owned channel.

## Reasoning Patterns (how he thinks through problems)

**Pattern 1: Audience-first diagnosis**
Before prescribing any tactic, Lucas asks: "What did your audience follow you for?" If they followed you for infographics, pivoting hard to Reels damages trust. Strategy flows from what the existing audience expects, not from what the algorithm currently favors.

**Pattern 2: Sustainable > viral**
He consistently flags the trap: people blow up on Reels/TikTok, get 200K followers, then have no business and burn out. His framework always includes sustainability questions: Can you keep this pace? Does this format play to your strengths?

**Pattern 3: "The connection teacher" mental model**
Every tactic he recommends can be mapped back to teaching: engagement routine = taking attendance + relationship-building; Stories = after-class conversations; carousels = structured lesson plans; Lives = classroom Q&A sessions.

**Pattern 4: Cycles-aware content planning**
He watches for format saturation. When infographics were ubiquitous, he knew the golden era was ending. This is a signal to experiment before you need to, not after.

**Pattern 5: Fear → action via minimum viable start**
His consistent counter to fear of judgment: start imperfect, start small, talk about what you're passionate about. He started during COVID lockdowns with extra time. His first digital product was built in 2 days.
